1. About the Game

Infinite Warfare is an online first-person shooter built for audio gameplay. Everything is conveyed through 3D positional audio and a self-voicing menu interface — no visuals required. Your goal is to survive, eliminate other players, capture territory, and climb the kill milestones.

You can play solo or pair with a duo partner to revive each other, share vehicles, and coordinate. The game features a deep weapon system with fire modes, attachments, holsters, and durability; a full item and equipment economy; drivable vehicles; and capturable underground bunkers.

2. Getting Started

2.1. Main Menu

  • Connect to the server — Log in and enter the lobby.
  • Register a new account — Create a username, password, and email.
  • Account management — Log in, authorize your account, or reset your password.
  • View the changelog — See what changed in recent builds.
  • Learn game sounds — Listen to and memorize key in-game sounds.
  • Options — Open the options menu (see below).
  • Check for updates — Download the latest version if one is available.
  • Exit the game — Quit to desktop.

2.3. Key Binding Editor

Open it from the options menu. It lists every rebindable action alongside its current key. Select an action, press Rebind selected, then press the new key. If that key is already used by another action, the two actions swap keys automatically so you never end up with a conflict. Reset all to defaults restores every binding. Bindings are saved to keyconfig.json; if that file is missing or corrupted the game rebuilds it from the defaults on launch. The default keys for every action are listed in Section 4.

2.4. The Lobby

After logging in you land in the lobby. From here you can:

  • Modes — Choose a game mode and deploy.
  • Account Info — View your account details.
  • Players — See who is online.
  • Killstreak Rewards — Review the kill-milestone rewards.
  • Leave Lobby — Disconnect to the main menu.
  • Press F1 at any time for the detailed player list.

When you deploy you receive a fresh random loadout: 1 melee weapon, 1 sidearm, and 1 primary, plus 2 health potions and 3 frag grenades, auto-assigned to quickbar slots 1–5.

5. Combat Mechanics

5.1. Fire Modes

Press Shift+A to cycle a weapon's available fire modes.

ModeAvailable onEffect
Semi-automaticMost weaponsOne shot per trigger pull.
BurstMany SMGs / riflesFires a 3-round burst per pull.
Full automaticSMGs, rifles, LMGs, some energyFires continuously while held.
ScattershotShotgunsWider pellet spread (+1 spread) at 0.7× damage per pellet.
FocusedEnergy weapons1.5× damage and 1.3× range, but consumes 3 charge per shot and can wear down durability (see 5.4).

5.2. Headshots

If you fire while aiming downward and your target is five or more tiles below you, the shot is a headshot: 1.5× damage, or 1.75× for sniper rifles. A "Headshot!" callout plays when one lands.

5.3. Shotgun Damage Dropoff

Shotguns deal full damage within the first half of their range. Past that, damage scales down smoothly to a floor of 0.75× (a 25% reduction) at maximum range — rewarding close-quarters fighting without hard-cutting longer shots.

5.4. Weapon Durability

Only energy weapons have durability, and they start at 100%. Each shot fired in focused mode has a 20% chance to lose 1–10% durability. At 0% the weapon breaks and is salvaged into 1–5 Nanomatic Components. Repair with a Nanomatic Multitool (see the Item Index). Firing in normal modes does not damage durability.

5.5. Melee

Melee weapons use no ammunition, cost 5 stamina per swing, and cannot be used from inside a vehicle. They hit hard at very short range — see the melee table in the Weapon Index.

5.6. Reloading & Ammunition

Press R to reload the drawn weapon and Shift+R to unload it. How ammunition behaves depends on the weapon:

  • Magazine weapons (pistols, rifles, SMGs) swap a whole magazine or clip on reload. A partially spent magazine you unload is kept as a partial and reads as capacity remaining — for example a partly used 9x19mm magazine shows as 15/17.
  • Individual-load weapons (revolvers, shotguns, the mortar, the crossbow) feed rounds from a belt, box, or quiver. Using the ammo item (Enter on it, or a quickbar slot) while the matching weapon is drawn asks how many rounds to load and scales the reload time to match — loading one shell is far quicker than loading a full tube.
  • Energy weapons swap power cells like magazines and show a charge percentage.
  • Fuel weapons (flamethrower, chainsaw) siphon fuel rather than swapping canisters. R tops the tank up to 100%, drawing only the fuel it needs and leaving any partly used canister as a partial. Using a fuel canister directly lets you fill the tank to a chosen percentage.

Using ammunition with no matching weapon drawn (or a magazine, which can't be hand-loaded) simply tells you which weapon it feeds and to press R.

7. Attachments & Holsters

7.1. Suppressors

Use a suppressor item from your inventory to fit it to your drawn weapon if compatible. A suppressed weapon shows a "silenced" prefix in the inventory and reduces its firing sound, at the cost of a slightly longer fire delay. Each weapon holds one muzzle attachment. Press Shift+Enter on a weapon to view its stats and remove attachments. Revolvers, shotguns, LMGs, energy, and special weapons cannot be suppressed.

9. Vehicles

Vehicles spawn randomly around the map. Press O within 5 tiles to open a door, then V to enter. Only passengers with their window down can fire; melee cannot be used from a vehicle. Vehicles take damage crashing into walls. When a vehicle is destroyed everyone inside is ejected and takes 25% health damage. Armor absorbs incoming damage fully, but each absorbed hit also chips the vehicle's own health by a quarter of that damage. Sitting in a vehicle restores +5 stamina every 15 seconds. Speed is listed in milliseconds per tile — lower is faster.

12.2. Credits

Credits are the in-game currency earned through combat. You earn 10–20 credits per kill, plus the milestone bonuses above. Credits are saved to your account and persist between sessions, but are lost and reset when you die. Check your balance from the lobby with the lobby_credits command.

13. Bunkers & HyperComp

Bunkers are capturable underground strongholds. Type /bunkers in chat to hear the locations on your current map.

  1. Find a bunker and its control panel (each has a ladder entrance and item spawns).
  2. Use a Bunker Key at the control panel to capture it.
  3. Once you own a bunker you can:
    • Fire the ballista in front of the bunker using Rocks (760 damage per bolt).
    • Place a Bunker Bomb at the control panel for a 2000-damage, 150-tile blast on a 14-second timer.
    • Flood the tunnels: fill 10 buckets at a water tile, pour them into the tunnel water tank, then press O.
  4. Capture a second bunker to receive a HyperComp, which lets you monitor intruders, toggle bunker alarms, and remotely detonate placed C4. You lose the HyperComp and release your bunkers when you die.
