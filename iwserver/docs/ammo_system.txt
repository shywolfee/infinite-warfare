Ammo System

Weapons use realistic magazine and ammo mechanics.

Reloading: Press R to reload. If you have both full and partial magazines, a menu lets you choose which to load.
Unloading: Press Shift+R to unload the current magazine back into your inventory.
Ammo check: Press A to hear rounds loaded, ammo type, and reserve count.

Magazine-fed weapons (pistols, rifles, SMGs) consume a magazine item from inventory when reloaded. Revolvers draw rounds from an ammo belt. Shotguns load individual shells from a shell box.

Using an ammunition item: Using a magazine, clip, or energy cell tells you which weapon it feeds so you can draw that weapon and press R. Using an ammo belt, shell box, loose mortar shell, or bolt quiver while the matching weapon is drawn loads rounds straight into it. You are asked how many rounds to load, and the reload takes a fraction of a full reload proportional to the number of rounds. Partial magazines and clips show capacity remaining, such as 15 of 17, rather than a percentage.

Laser weapons each use their own power containment cell (the Volt Sidearm uses a volt cell, the Pulse Repeater a pulse cell, the Photon Carbine a photon cell, the Particle Lance a lance capacitor, and the Nova Cannon a nova core) and display charge as a percentage. These cells are not interchangeable between weapons.

Fuel weapons (Flamethrower and Chainsaw) siphon fuel from their canisters rather than swapping them. Pressing R tops the tank up to 100%, consuming only the fuel needed and turning a partly used canister into a partial one. Using a fuel canister directly lets you fill the tank to a chosen percentage. Fuel is shown as a percentage.

Weapons that share the same caliber and magazine capacity share magazine items. For example, the Glock 17 and Beretta 92FS both use 9x19mm magazines.
